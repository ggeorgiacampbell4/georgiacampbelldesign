Overview

This project tasked our team with designing a user-centred assistive device for individuals with Multiple Sclerosis (MS) who experience impaired hand dexterity. Our specific focus was on earring backing application: a small, precise task that becomes extremely challenging when fine motor control is reduced.

Design Process

We followed an iterative mechanical design process grounded in human factors principles. The device had to address real usability constraints: safety (no sharp edges or fragile parts), ergonomics (comfortable grip for users with limited grip strength), and independence (able to be used single-handedly).

CAD modelling was done in Autodesk Inventor, with multiple prototyping rounds via 3D printing. Each iteration was evaluated against human-factors risk criteria and usability feedback collected from end users.

Key Contributions

  • Led iterative CAD development in Autodesk Inventor through multiple design cycles
  • Applied human-factors risk analysis to identify and mitigate usability hazards
  • Coordinated documentation, design reviews, and the final demonstration
  • Validated final prototype with end-user testing and feedback
  • Presented at the iBioMed Year-End Design Showcase
